# Your first 30 days in the Netherlands
## The checklist — Dutch Survival Hacks

---

## Week 1 — Get registered

The first thing you do is register. Everything else depends on it.

- [ ] **Book a gemeente appointment** — do this before or as soon as you arrive
  - Find your city's website (e.g. amsterdam.nl, denhaag.nl, rotterdam.nl)
  - Look for "inschrijven" or "first registration"
  - Bring: valid passport, rental contract or proof of address
  - Note: if you're staying 4+ months, registration is mandatory within 5 days

- [ ] **Register at the gemeente**
  - You'll automatically receive your **BSN** (citizen service number) at this appointment
  - Without a BSN you can't open a bank account, get health insurance or start work

- [ ] **Give your BSN to your employer**
  - They need it before your first salary payment

---

## Week 2 — DigiD + bank account

- [ ] **Apply for DigiD** at digid.nl
  - You need your BSN and a Dutch address
  - A letter arrives by post within 5–7 days — don't lose it
  - DigiD is your key to every Dutch government website

- [ ] **Open a Dutch bank account**
  - ABN AMRO, ING and Rabobank all work with new expats
  - Bring: passport, BSN, proof of address
  - Some banks require an appointment — book online in advance
  - Tip: Bunq and Revolut work without a BSN if you need something fast

---

## Week 3 — Health insurance + daily life

- [ ] **Get health insurance (zorgverzekering)** — this is mandatory
  - You have 4 months from registration to arrange this
  - Basic package costs roughly €135–165/month
  - Compare insurers at zorgwijzer.nl or independer.nl
  - Biggest insurers: Zilveren Kruis, VGZ, Menzis, CZ, DSW

- [ ] **Check if you qualify for zorgtoeslag** (healthcare allowance)
  - Income-based subsidy that reduces your monthly premium
  - Apply at toeslagen.nl — you need your DigiD for this (Week 4)

- [ ] **Register with a GP (huisarts)**
  - Find one near you at huisartszoeker.nl
  - Walk in or call to register — bring your BSN and address
  - Do this before you need one, not when you're already sick

- [ ] **Get an OV-chipkaart** (public transport card)
  - Order at ov-chipkaart.nl or pick one up at a train station
  - Or just tap your contactless bank card — works on most trains, trams and buses

---

## Week 4 — Tax & benefits

- [ ] **Activate your DigiD**
  - The letter from Week 2 should have arrived by now
  - Go to digid.nl to activate with the code in the letter

- [ ] **Ask HR about the 30% ruling** — do this now, not later
  - A tax benefit that lets you receive 30% of your salary tax-free
  - You may qualify if: you were recruited from abroad, your salary is above ~€46,000 gross, and you lived more than 150km from the Dutch border when hired
  - Your employer must apply — and the deadline is 4 months after your start date
  - Miss the window and you lose it

- [ ] **Check your toeslagen (allowances)** at toeslagen.nl
  - Zorgtoeslag — healthcare allowance (most expats qualify)
  - Huurtoeslag — rent allowance (depends on income and rent)
  - You need DigiD to apply

- [ ] **Register at belastingdienst.nl** if you're self-employed
  - Also worth checking if you have income from outside the Netherlands

## Dutch terms you'll keep running into

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **BSN** | Burgerservicenummer — your citizen service number. Like a social security number. You need it for everything. |
| **DigiD** | Your digital identity for all Dutch government websites. One login for all services. |
| **Gemeente** | Your local municipality office. Where you register and arrange official things. |
| **Huisarts** | GP / general practitioner. Register before you need one. |
| **Zorgverzekering** | Health insurance. Mandatory. Fines if you don't have it. |
| **Zorgtoeslag** | Healthcare allowance — a monthly subsidy on your insurance premium. |
| **OV-chipkaart** | Public transport card. Used on trains, trams, buses and metro. |
| **Belastingdienst** | The Dutch tax authority. belastingdienst.nl has an English section. |
| **30%-regeling** | The 30% ruling — a tax benefit for some expats hired from abroad. |
| **Toeslagen** | Government allowances (healthcare, rent, childcare). Check if you qualify. |
| **Inschrijven** | To register. You'll see this word everywhere. |
