Exchanging your foreign driving licence in the Netherlands

Whether you can simply swap your foreign licence for a Dutch one — or have to start from scratch — depends entirely on where your licence was issued. Here's the full picture, by country.

EU and EEA licence holders: you're fine

If your driving licence was issued in an EU or EEA country (including Germany, France, Spain, Italy, Belgium, and so on), you can drive in the Netherlands on your existing licence without doing anything. It's valid throughout the EU.

You can voluntarily exchange it for a Dutch licence — which makes sense if you're staying long-term, as it simplifies things later (especially if your foreign licence expires). But it's not required.

To exchange voluntarily, go to your gemeente (Rijbewijsdesk) with your current licence, passport, BSN, and a passport photo. The fee is around €40.

Countries with a direct exchange agreement

The Netherlands has bilateral agreements with a number of non-EU countries, allowing you to exchange your foreign licence for a Dutch one without taking a new test. This list includes:

  • United Kingdom
  • Australia
  • Japan
  • South Korea
  • Singapore
  • Taiwan
  • Isle of Man
  • Jersey and Guernsey
  • Aruba, Curaçao, Sint Maarten
  • Switzerland and Liechtenstein (EEA)

The list is updated periodically — always verify on the RDW website (rdw.nl) or CBR (cbr.nl) before you start the process, as agreements can change.

UK licence holders (post-Brexit)

The Netherlands and the UK have a bilateral exchange agreement in place, so UK licence holders can exchange directly without retaking tests. You have a grace period after registering — check current rules on rdw.nl as the exact timeframe can vary.

South African licence holders

South Africa has an exchange agreement with the Netherlands. You can swap your SA licence for a Dutch one directly — no theory or practical test needed. Bring your original licence (not a copy), proof of residence, BSN, passport, and passport photo to your gemeente.

Countries without an exchange agreement

If your country is not on the exchange list, you'll need to go through the full Dutch driving licence process. This means:

  • Theory exam (theorie-examen) — multiple choice, available in English at CBR exam centres
  • Practical driving lessons — required before you can sit the practical exam; the number depends on your experience
  • Practical exam (rijexamen) — taken at a CBR location

Countries in this category include India, the United States, Canada, and most of Africa and Asia not listed above. Having years of driving experience doesn't exempt you from the test — you still go through the full process.

India

Indian licence holders cannot exchange directly. You'll need to take both the theory and practical exam. On the plus side, the Dutch theory exam is available in English and is very passable with a few weeks of preparation using the official CBR practice materials. Driving lessons with an instructor familiar with expats can significantly reduce the number of lessons needed.

United States

There is no federal exchange agreement between the US and the Netherlands, so American licence holders also need to take the full Dutch test. This catches many American expats by surprise — even with decades of driving experience. The theory exam in English is the first step; budget time and money for lessons and exams.

The exchange process (for countries with agreements)

  1. Make an appointment at your gemeente's driving licence desk (rijbewijsdesk). Not all municipalities handle this — check your local gemeente website
  2. Bring: original foreign driving licence, valid passport or ID, BSN, one passport photo (35x45mm), and payment for the fee (around €40)
  3. Your foreign licence is taken and sent back to the issuing country — you won't get it back. Make sure you won't need it in your home country before you hand it in
  4. Your Dutch licence arrives by post within a few weeks

How long can you drive on a foreign licence?

If you're registered in the Netherlands:

  • EU/EEA licence: valid indefinitely while living here
  • Exchange agreement country: typically valid for a limited period after registration (often 6–12 months) — exchange it promptly
  • No agreement: your foreign licence remains technically valid for driving, but you should start the Dutch process quickly. Some insurers may also ask about your licence status

The 30% ruling perk

If you have the 30% ruling, you have simplified access to exchange your foreign driving licence — one of the smaller but genuine perks attached to the ruling. Ask your HR or tax advisor about how this works in your specific situation.

Common questions

Can I drive immediately after arriving?
Yes — your foreign licence is valid for driving in the Netherlands from day one. The question is how long that validity lasts before you need to exchange or qualify.

What if my foreign licence has expired?
An expired foreign licence cannot be exchanged. If your home country licence has expired, you'll need to go through the full Dutch process.

Do I need a Dutch licence to rent a car?
Most rental companies accept any valid foreign licence for short-term rentals. For longer-term leases or company cars, a Dutch or EU licence is usually preferred.

Where can I find the current list of exchange countries?
Always check rdw.nl (the Dutch vehicle authority) for the most up-to-date list. The CBR (cbr.nl) is also useful for information on theory and practical exams.
