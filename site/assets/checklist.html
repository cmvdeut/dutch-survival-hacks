Your first 30 days in the Netherlands

The checklist — Dutch Survival Hacks

Week 1 — Get registered

The first thing you do is register. Everything else depends on it.

  • Book a gemeente appointment — do this before or as soon as you arrive
    • Find your city's website (e.g. amsterdam.nl, denhaag.nl, rotterdam.nl)
    • Look for "inschrijven" or "first registration"
    • Bring: valid passport, rental contract or proof of address
    • Note: if you're staying 4+ months, registration is mandatory within 5 days
  • Register at the gemeente
    • You'll automatically receive your BSN (citizen service number) at this appointment
    • Without a BSN you can't open a bank account, get health insurance or start work
  • Give your BSN to your employer — they need it before your first salary payment

Week 2 — DigiD + bank account

  • Apply for DigiD at digid.nl
    • You need your BSN and a Dutch address
    • A letter arrives by post within 5–7 days — don't lose it
    • DigiD is your key to every Dutch government website
  • Open a Dutch bank account
    • ABN AMRO, ING and Rabobank all work with new expats
    • Bring: passport, BSN, proof of address
    • Some banks require an appointment — book online in advance
    • Tip: Bunq and Revolut work without a BSN if you need something fast

Week 3 — Health insurance + daily life

  • Get health insurance (zorgverzekering) — this is mandatory
    • You have 4 months from registration to arrange this
    • Basic package costs roughly €135–165/month
    • Compare insurers at zorgwijzer.nl or independer.nl
    • Biggest insurers: Zilveren Kruis, VGZ, Menzis, CZ, DSW
  • Check if you qualify for zorgtoeslag (healthcare allowance) — income-based subsidy; apply at toeslagen.nl (you need DigiD in Week 4)
  • Register with a GP (huisarts) — find one at huisartszoeker.nl; bring BSN and address
  • Get an OV-chipkaart — order at ov-chipkaart.nl or use contactless bank card on most transport

Week 4 — Tax & benefits

  • Activate your DigiD — use the code from the letter that arrived in Week 2
  • Ask HR about the 30% ruling — do this now. Tax benefit for many expats; employer must apply within 4 months of start date.
  • Check toeslagen at toeslagen.nl — zorgtoeslag, huurtoeslag; you need DigiD
  • Register at belastingdienst.nl if you're self-employed or have income from abroad
